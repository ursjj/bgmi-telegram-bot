## Step 4: Turn Your Local Folder into a Git Repository

### 1. Link the Folder to GitHub Desktop
* Open the **GitHub Desktop** application you installed.
* Click on **File** in the top left menu bar, then click **Add Local Repository...**
* A window pops up. Click the **Choose...** button.
* Browse to your Desktop, click on your `bgmi-telegram-bot` folder, and click **Select Folder**.

### 2. Create the Repository Structure
* You will see a blue warning notice saying the directory is not a Git repository yet. Click the blue text link inside that warning that says **create a repository**.
* A settings window will open up. Fill it out exactly like this:
  * **Name:** `bgmi-telegram-bot`
  * **Git Ignore:** Click the dropdown menu, type **Python**, and select it.
  * Leave everything else exactly as it is.
* Click the blue **Create Repository** button at the bottom of the window.

## Step 5: "Commit" and Push Your Code to the Cloud

### 1. Save Your Progress (Commit)
* Look at the left-hand column in GitHub Desktop. You will see your files listed with green checkmarks next to them.
* Move your eyes down to the bottom left corner of the app window where it says **Summary (required)**.
* Click inside that box and type: `My first bot upload`
* Click the blue **Commit to main** button directly beneath your text.

### 2. Send It to GitHub's Servers (Publish)
* Look at the very top center or top right of the GitHub Desktop app. You will see a button labeled **Publish repository**. Click it.
* A small panel will slide into view. 
* **Keep your code safe:** Make sure the checkbox labeled **Keep this code private** is **Checked (ON)**. This ensures nobody else on the internet can see your work.
* Click the final blue **Publish Repository** button at the bottom of that small panel.
